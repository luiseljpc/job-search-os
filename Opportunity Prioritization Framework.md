**Opportunity Prioritization Framework**

**Purpose**

This framework defines how to turn a batch of evaluated job
opportunities into a more actionable pursuit order.

It is designed to solve a practical problem:

a large opportunity-evaluation table may be rigorous and
information-rich, but still too dense to make starting decisions easy.

This framework adds a second-layer prioritization view that helps
identify:

- where to start

- which roles are strongest practical pursue cases

- which roles are easier wins

- which stretch roles are worth selective pursuit

- which opportunities appear attractive across multiple lenses

It does **not** define:

- fit scoring logic

- desirability scoring logic

- opportunity score calculation

- resume routing logic

- application-generation logic

It is a prioritization layer, not a replacement for the underlying
evaluation system.

**What this framework owns**

This document is the single source of truth for:

- pursuit-bucket definitions

- placement rules for each bucket

- overlap rules

- cross-bucket-standout rules

- prioritization output format

**Inputs**

This framework should be used only after the opportunity evaluation
workflow has already been run.

Use the evaluated opportunity set as the input.

Primary inputs may include:

- Opportunity Score

- Desirability Score

- Fit Score

- Next Move

- Company

- Title

- Compensation

- Work Arrangement

- Key Responsibilities

- What the Company Likely Needs from This Role

- Strong Match Areas

- Gaps / Weak Areas

The framework may also use supporting judgment from:

- target-lane relevance

- fit bucket

- confidence notes / flags

- full JD context when needed

**Core principle**

This framework exists to improve actionability, not to create a second
scoring system.

It should translate the evaluated opportunity set into a more useful
starting order for pursuit.

It should not invent a new numeric model.

It should not override the underlying evaluation frameworks.

It should not hide weak fit behind excitement.

It should not flatten meaningful distinctions between easy wins, strong
fits, and selective stretches.

**Bucket structure**

Use these 5 buckets.

A role may appear in more than one bucket when each placement is
genuinely justified and adds distinct decision value.

Do not force exclusivity.

Do not place a role into multiple buckets casually.

Multiple placement is justified only when the role clearly satisfies the
criteria of each included bucket.

**1. Highest Excitement / Priority**

Use for the opportunities that best combine:

- strong fit or credible fit

- strong upside

- attractive compensation or trajectory

- real strategic value

- genuine energy / excitement

This bucket is not just "highest score."

It is the highest-priority starting group.

A role in this bucket should feel like a role worth serious near-term
attention.

**2. Core Fit / Strong Pursue**

Use for roles where the substantive alignment is strong and defensible.

These are opportunities where:

- the fit case is real

- the background maps meaningfully to the role

- the role is a good use of application effort

- the candidacy is not mainly wishful or speculative

These roles may or may not be among the most exciting.

This bucket represents the core center of gravity of the search.

**3. Low-Hanging Fruit**

Use for roles where the value story is relatively easy to tell and
employer recognition of fit is likely faster.

These are opportunities where:

- the sell is cleaner

- tailoring is lighter

- the employer is more likely to quickly understand the relevance of the
  profile

- the role may be efficiently pursue-worthy even if not the most
  exciting

This bucket does not mean low quality.

It means lower-friction opportunity with clearer market legibility.

**4. AI Stretch but Plausible**

Use for future-facing or AI-adjacent roles where the stretch is real,
but the candidacy is still credibly arguable.

These are opportunities where:

- the role has meaningful AI adjacency or future-facing value

- there is some credible bridge from the current profile

- the stretch is selective, not fantasy

- pursuit may be worthwhile for positioning, optionality, or trajectory
  reasons

Use conservatively.

Do not place roles here just because they contain fashionable AI
language.

**5. High-Upside Stretch**

Use for roles that could materially improve trajectory, brand,
compensation, or future positioning, but where the transfer leap is more
substantial.

These are opportunities where:

- upside is meaningfully higher than average

- the role could change the trajectory of the search

- the leap is real enough that pursuit should be selective

- the opportunity is attractive partly because of future leverage, not
  just present fit

This bucket is for selective bets.

It should not absorb every attractive role.

**Overlap rules**

A role may appear in multiple buckets.

That is allowed because the buckets reflect different kinds of decision
value.

Examples of valid overlap:

- Highest Excitement / Priority + Core Fit / Strong Pursue

- Core Fit / Strong Pursue + Low-Hanging Fruit

- Highest Excitement / Priority + High-Upside Stretch

- AI Stretch but Plausible + High-Upside Stretch

A role should appear in multiple buckets only when:

- each bucket reflects a distinct and true reason to prioritize or
  consider the role

- the overlap improves decision quality

- the overlap is not just a flattering restatement of general
  attractiveness

Avoid lazy overlap.

If a role appears in multiple buckets, the reasoning should be real, not
merely positive.

**Cross-Bucket Standouts**

After bucket placement, identify roles that appear in 2 or more buckets.

These are cross-bucket standouts.

Their significance is:

they combine multiple reasons for pursuit and may deserve earlier
attention than roles appearing in only one bucket.

Use the following groupings:

**2-bucket overlaps**

Roles that appear in exactly 2 buckets

**3+ bucket overlaps**

Roles that appear in 3 or more buckets

A role appearing in 3 or more buckets is not automatically better than
every other role, but it is a strong signal that the opportunity
deserves closer priority attention.

**Output format**

When this framework is used, present the prioritization output
**bucket-first**, not role-first.

Use this order:

**Opportunity Prioritization**

**Highest Excitement / Priority**

- Company --- Title

- Company --- Title

**Core Fit / Strong Pursue**

- Company --- Title

- Company --- Title

**Low-Hanging Fruit**

- Company --- Title

- Company --- Title

**AI Stretch but Plausible**

- Company --- Title

- Company --- Title

**High-Upside Stretch**

- Company --- Title

- Company --- Title

Then add:

**Cross-Bucket Standouts**

**Appearing in 2 buckets**

- Company --- Title\
  Appears in: \[Bucket 1\]; \[Bucket 2\]

**Appearing in 3+ buckets**

- Company --- Title\
  Appears in: \[Bucket 1\]; \[Bucket 2\]; \[Bucket 3\]

**Output rules**

- organize by bucket, not by role

- list only company and title unless more detail is requested

- do not repeat long rationales under every bullet

- do not invent a new score

- do not treat overlap as its own sixth bucket

- use overlap only as a summary signal after the bucketed view

- optimize for actionability and scanning

**Use case guidance**

This framework is primarily for:

- multi-opportunity evaluation runs

- large opportunity tables

- deciding where to start applying

It is most useful for batches such as:

- 10 opportunities

- 20 opportunities

- 50 opportunities

It is not required for every single-role evaluation unless specifically
requested.

**Final rule**

The purpose of this framework is to help answer:

**Where should I start, and which roles deserve disproportionate
attention?**

It should make the evaluation output easier to act on, not more
complicated.
