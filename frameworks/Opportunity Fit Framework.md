**Opportunity Fit Framework**

**Purpose**

This framework defines how well the candidate matches a job opportunity
based on the strongest truthful interpretation supported by the
candidate's evidence pack.

It answers:

**How strong is the candidate for this role?**

This framework is the single source of truth for:

- fit sub-scores

- Best Case for My Fit

- fit confidence note

- fit flags

- bucket classification

It does **not** own compensation logic.

**Core scoring structure**

Total Fit Score = **10**

The score is composed of four sub-scores:

- Functional Match = /3

- Seniority Match = /2

- Qualification Match = /3

- Evidence Strength = /2

**Primary evidence base**

**Primary evidence**

Use these first:

- Resume Set

- Strengths and Capabilities Profile

- Accomplishments Inventory

- Differentiator Summary

**Secondary support**

Use these secondarily:

- Resume Routing Guide

- Positioning and Bullet Bank

Secondary support can strengthen framing, but should not override harder
evidence.

**1. Functional Match (/3)**

**Definition**

How closely the actual function of the role matches the work I have
strongest evidence for.

**Scoring**

- **3** = Strong direct functional overlap

- **2** = Strong adjacent overlap with credible transfer

- **1** = Partial overlap, but visible functional stretch

- **0** = Core function is materially outside the candidate's profile

**Strong positive signals**

Evidence of:

- strategic reframing

- insight-to-action translation

- audience / consumer / market understanding

- cross-functional strategic support

- capability building

- reusable asset or system creation

- ambiguity handling

- commercially useful interpretation

**Caution**

Do not inflate fit just because the title sounds attractive.

Judge the real function.

**2. Seniority Match (/2)**

**Definition**

How well the candidate's demonstrated scope and operating level matches
the likely level of the role.

**Scoring**

- **2** = Level looks credibly matched

- **1** = Some level stretch or ambiguity, but still plausible

- **0** = Clear level mismatch

**Interpretation rules**

- Treat ambiguous seniority conservatively.

- Distinguish between title inflation and real scope.

- Do not under-credit senior strategic judgment just because the
  background is unconventional.

**3. Qualification Match (/3)**

**Definition**

How well the candidate's background aligns with the stated required and
preferred qualifications.

**Scoring**

- **3** = Strong coverage of core qualifications

- **2** = Good coverage with some manageable gaps

- **1** = Several visible gaps, though candidacy is still arguable

- **0** = Core requirements are not credibly supported

**Interpretation rules**

- Separate true requirement gaps from weak signaling.

- Do not credit me with technical depth I do not have.

- Do not over-penalize for lacking exact category language when
  transferable strategic evidence is strong.

**4. Evidence Strength (/2)**

**Definition**

How strong the supporting proof is for the most important parts of the
candidacy.

**Scoring**

- **2** = Strong, relevant proof exists

- **1** = Some relevant proof exists, but coverage is uneven

- **0** = Evidence is weak, indirect, or too thin

**Strong Match**

Each evaluation should produce a short summary of the clearest alignment
areas.

**Format**

Short phrase list or one short compact line.

**Rule**

Capture only the strongest real alignment.

**Gaps / Weak Areas**

Each evaluation should produce a short summary of the most meaningful
visible weaknesses.

**Rule**

Include only meaningful gaps.

Do not invent weaknesses for symmetry.

**Weakness Type**

**Allowed values**

- None

- Signaling Gap

- Domain Gap

- Tool / Technical Gap

- Level Gap

- Functional Gap

- Multiple Weakness Types

**Purpose**

This distinguishes:

- presentational issues

- tolerable stretch factors

- materially weakening mismatches

**Best Case for My Fit**

Each fit evaluation must include one short line answering:

**Why might they seriously consider the candidate for this role?**

**Purpose**

This forces the engine to articulate the strongest plausible case for
candidacy.

**Format**

One short sentence only.

**Length**

Maximum **20 words**

**Rule**

This should be informed especially by:

- Differentiator Summary

- Strengths and Capabilities Profile

- Accomplishments Inventory

- relevant resume evidence

**It should not be**

- generic praise

- a repeat of the score

- a full rationale paragraph

**Fit Confidence Note**

Each fit score must include a short audit note.

**Format**

A brief phrase, not a paragraph.

**Good examples**

- Strong strategy match, minor signaling gaps

- Good overlap, weaker direct AI/tool background

- Strong evidence, but scope slightly stretched

- Interesting role, but functional mismatch too high

**Unknown / Data Risk Flag**

Each fit score must include a flag.

**Allowed values**

- None

- Qualification Ambiguity

- Seniority Unclear

- Scope Unclear

- Evidence Low Confidence

- Multiple Unknowns

**Rule**

Unknowns should be handled conservatively, not optimistically.

If the JD is vague or the evidence does not clearly support a
requirement, that uncertainty should reduce confidence.

**Bucket classification**

Each opportunity must be classified as one of the following:

- Strong Fit

- Stretch but Plausible

- Distraction

**Interpretation**

**Strong Fit\**
High overall fit, no major functional mismatch, and weaknesses are
mostly manageable or presentational.

**Stretch but Plausible\**
Real strengths exist and candidacy is defensible, but there are visible
gaps or level questions.

**Distraction\**
Fit depends too much on wishful thinking, or the mismatch is too
significant relative to better opportunities.

**Interpretation rule**

The fit score should reflect the strength of the current candidacy,
based on the current evidence pack and the strongest truthful framing it
supports.

This means:

- give real credit for transferable strategic and research strengths

- distinguish true mismatches from weak signaling

- remain conservative where technical, functional, or level gaps are
  central

- avoid flattering stretch roles just because they are attractive

**Summary formula**

**Fit Score = Functional Match (/3) + Seniority Match (/2) +
Qualification Match (/3) + Evidence Strength (/2)**

Maximum = **10**

**Practical scoring instruction**

When scoring fit:

1.  identify the real function, likely level, and core requirements

2.  compare the role against the primary evidence base first

3.  score each of the four fit subcomponents

4.  write:

    - Strong Match

    - Gaps / Weak Areas

    - Weakness Type

    - Best Case for My Fit

    - Fit Confidence Note

    - Unknown / Data Risk Flag

    - Bucket

5.  score conservatively where technical, functional, or level mismatch
    is central

**Final interpretation rule**

This framework measures candidacy credibility, not opportunity
attractiveness.

A role can be:

- high fit, low desirability

- low fit, high desirability

- high on both

- low on both

Do not let attraction to the role inflate the fit score.
