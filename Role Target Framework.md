**Role Target Framework**

**Purpose**

This framework defines the types of roles the job search system should
prioritize.

It answers:

**Is this role meaningfully in-bounds for the search, adjacent but
plausible, or likely a distraction?**

This framework is the single source of truth for:

- target role lanes

- target tiers

- role-category relevance

It does **not** define:

- desirability scoring

- fit scoring

- final opportunity score

- resume-tailoring logic

**Core principle**

The goal is not to pursue every role one could possibly argue for.

The goal is to prioritize roles where the candidate's background is most
competitively valuable relative to:

- what the role actually requires

- the kind of signal the market will reward

- the direction the candidate wants to build toward

This framework should keep the search focused.

**Tier structure**

**Tier 1 --- Core target roles**

These are the strongest role categories for the current search.

They are the best use of evaluation and application energy.

**Tier 1A --- Strategic Insights / Consumer Strategy / Audience
Strategy**

Best when the role asks for:

- consumer, audience, or market understanding

- strategic interpretation

- insight-to-action translation

- business decision support

- stakeholder-facing recommendations

- framing complex market or audience questions

Typical titles may include:

- Director, Strategy & Insights

- Consumer Strategy Lead

- Audience Strategy Director

- Strategic Insights Lead

- Insights Director

- Consumer Planning / Strategic Planning roles with real business
  influence

**Tier 1B --- Research Innovation / Capability Building / Insight
Operations**

Best when the role asks for:

- improving how insight work gets done

- building frameworks, systems, or repeatable workflows

- research innovation

- methodology shaping

- capability building

- better decision-support infrastructure

Typical titles may include:

- Research Innovation Lead

- Insights Operations Lead

- Capability Building Lead

- Strategic Research / Insights Enablement roles

- roles improving insight quality, speed, or organizational usefulness

**Tier 1C --- AI-Adjacent Strategy / Future-Facing Strategy Roles**

Best when the role asks for:

- strategic thinking in future-facing environments

- AI-enabled decision support

- AI-adjacent innovation or transformation work

- translating ambiguity into useful frameworks, systems, or strategic
  action

- helping organizations interpret new technological shifts

Typical titles may include:

- AI Strategy roles with strong strategic rather than deeply technical
  requirements

- innovation strategy roles with AI adjacency

- future-of-work / emerging technology strategy roles

- operator or strategy roles where AI leverage is meaningful but deep
  technical execution is not central

**Tier 2 --- Adjacent but Plausible Roles**

These can still be worth evaluating, but they require more selectivity.

They should usually be pursued when at least one of the following is
true:

- the desirability is unusually strong

- the fit case is still quite defensible

- the role offers clear strategic upside

- the title is adjacent but the actual function is still close to the
  candidate's strengths

**Tier 2A --- Broader Strategy Roles**

Examples:

- growth strategy

- brand strategy with real analytic or consumer depth

- business strategy roles with strong research / interpretation
  components

- planning roles where strategic synthesis matters more than purely
  agency signaling

**Tier 2B --- Product-Adjacent / Innovation-Adjacent Roles**

Examples:

- roles that sit near product, innovation, or operations but are still
  primarily strategic, interpretive, or research-driven

- roles where framing, prioritization, insight, and structured thinking
  matter more than deep product management or technical ownership

**Tier 2C --- Commercial Insight / Category / Market Intelligence
Roles**

Examples:

- category intelligence

- market intelligence

- portfolio intelligence

- competitive intelligence

- commercial strategy roles grounded in analysis and interpretation

**Tier 3 --- Usually low-priority or distraction roles**

These are usually poor targets unless an unusual case makes them more
plausible than they first appear.

**Typical Tier 3 categories**

- deeply technical AI or product roles requiring hands-on execution not
  supported by the evidence pack

- pure research-method specialist roles where hard-method depth is
  central and not credibly supported

- sales, partnerships, or revenue roles with little strategic/insight
  overlap

- generalist operational roles where the strongest parts of the
  candidate's profile are underused

- roles that are mostly transactional and weak on both strategic value
  and candidate fit

**On-target relevance categories**

When evaluating a role, classify its target-lane relevance as one of the
following:

**1. Directly On Target**

The role clearly sits in a Tier 1 lane and the function matches the
strongest parts of the profile.

**2. Adjacent but Plausible**

The role sits in Tier 2 or near the edge of Tier 1, and a credible case
can still be made.

**3. Likely Off Target**

The role falls mostly outside the defined lanes or depends too heavily
on wishful interpretation.

**Interpretation rules**

**1. Judge the real function, not just the title**

Titles can mislead.

Use the actual work described in the posting to decide which lane the
role belongs to.

**2. A prestigious company does not make an off-target role on-target**

Company quality may affect desirability, but not role-category
relevance.

**3. AI language should be interpreted carefully**

A role is not automatically in the AI-adjacent lane just because it
mentions AI.

It should only count as AI-adjacent when:

- AI is materially relevant to the role

- the role still fits the actual profile

- the case does not depend on pretending to have deeper technical
  execution than is supported

**4. Adjacent roles should still clear a real plausibility bar**

The point of Tier 2 is to allow intelligent flexibility, not drift.

**5. This framework guides search focus, not final pursue/skip decisions
by itself**

A role can be on-target but still low-desirability or low-fit.\
A role can be adjacent but still worth pursuing if the overall case is
strong enough.

**Practical use rule**

Use this framework early in the evaluation process to answer:

- Is this role in the right lane?

- Is this a core target or an adjacent case?

- Is this likely a distraction?

Then use:

- the Desirability Framework for attractiveness

- the Fit Framework for candidacy credibility

- the Score Framework for overall triage

**Final interpretation rule**

This framework exists to keep the search focused on strong opportunity
types.

It should reduce:

- random drift

- overreaction to attractive titles

- wasted time on weak-category roles

It should increase:

- search discipline

- category clarity

- strategic consistency
