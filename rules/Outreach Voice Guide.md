**Outreach Voice Guide**

**Purpose**

This document defines the writing voice and style the AI should use when
generating role-specific LinkedIn outreach messages for Luis.

The goal is to produce outreach that feels:

- relevant

- concise

- professional

- human

- strategically grounded

- easy to send without embarrassment

The messages should sound like a thoughtful, credible candidate making a
specific and legitimate connection to the role, not like a generic
networking template.

**1. Core voice qualities**

The outreach voice should be:

- **direct**

- **brief**

- **clear**

- **strategic**

- **polite**

- **grounded**

- **lightly warm, but restrained**

- **confident without sounding entitled**

The tone should feel mature, composed, and natural.

**2. Overall tone**

The message should not sound like:

- a networking script

- a mass outreach template

- a sales pitch

- a fan message

- a desperate request for help

- a generic "just wanted to connect" note

It should sound like:

- a short professional note

- written by someone with a real reason for reaching out

- who understands the role or team context

- and who is respectful of the other person's time

**3. Emotional tone**

The emotional tone should be measured.

Preferred tone:

- interested

- respectful

- purposeful

- calm

Avoid:

- gushiness

- flattery

- pleading

- overfamiliarity

- fake enthusiasm

- performative passion

**Rule**

Interest should come through the relevance of the message, not through
exaggerated emotional language.

**4. What the message should do**

A strong outreach message should usually do four things:

1.  establish a credible reason for reaching out

2.  show brief relevance to the role or team

3.  make a light, appropriate ask

4.  remain easy to respond to

The message should not try to do too much.

**5. Length preference**

The message should be **short**.

Target length:

- usually **3 to 5 sentences**

- generally under **100 words**

- occasionally up to **120 words** if needed

The default should be concise.

**6. Message style**

The writing should be:

- compact

- readable

- natural

- not stiff

- not overly polished to the point of sounding artificial

Preferred style characteristics:

- simple sentence structure

- specific relevance

- minimal filler

- no jargon for its own sake

- quick path to the point

**7. What to avoid**

Avoid language that feels:

- generic

- overeager

- needy

- vague

- too flattering

- too "networking-bro"

- too formal and robotic

**Avoid phrases like:**

- "I hope this message finds you well"

- "I would love to pick your brain"

- "I am so passionate about this opportunity"

- "I would be honored to connect"

- "I know you are busy, but..."

- "I wanted to reach out because your background is so inspiring"

- "I'd be grateful for any advice you can offer" unless very
  specifically grounded

**Avoid moves like:**

- overexplaining your story

- repeating your full qualifications

- making a heavy ask in the first message

- sounding like you want a favor from a stranger without earning
  relevance

- pretending familiarity that does not exist

**8. Contact-type adaptation**

The message should adapt based on contact type.

**Recruiter at the company**

Tone:

- direct

- concise

- role-focused

- lightly professional

Goal:

- express relevant interest

- signal fit

- put yourself on their radar

**Hiring Manager**

Tone:

- slightly more strategic

- more role-aware

- respectful and specific

Goal:

- show why your background is relevant

- make a credible contribution-oriented connection

- not overreach

**Adjacent Leader**

Tone:

- thoughtful

- role-aware

- slightly more contextual

Goal:

- show relevant alignment

- signal intelligent interest

- sometimes ask a light question or invite a brief conversation

**Potential Teammate / Functional Peer**

Tone:

- lighter

- more conversational

- still respectful

Goal:

- seek insight, not force advocacy

- ask a small, easy-to-answer question if appropriate

**Warm / Semi-Warm Connection**

Tone:

- more natural

- slightly more direct

- relational if truly justified

Goal:

- acknowledge the connection naturally

- move quickly to why you're reaching out

**9. Ask calibration**

The ask should be light.

Good asks:

- brief conversation

- quick perspective

- whether the role/team is being handled by a certain function

- whether they'd be open to a short exchange

- whether there's anything especially important to understand about the
  role

Avoid:

- immediate referral requests to strangers

- sending your whole background in message one

- asking for too much time

- acting as if the contact owes you help

**Rule**

The first message should open a door, not force a decision.

**10. Relationship to application strategy**

The message should be tied to the actual role and application context.

It should reflect:

- why this person is relevant

- why the role is relevant

- what angle makes sense for Luis's candidacy

It should not sound like a standalone generic networking message that
could have been sent to anyone.

**11. Relationship to voice and evidence**

The outreach must remain grounded in:

- the candidate evidence pack

- the full JD

- the contact's role, if known

- the outreach objective

The AI must not:

- invent connections

- invent shared history

- invent deep familiarity with the person's work

- overstate fit

- sound more intimate than the relationship warrants

The message should remain consistent with Luis's broader tone:\
direct, strategic, credible, and low-fluff.

**12. Candidate-specific style notes**

Luis's preferred outreach tone tends toward:

- clear thinking

- direct language

- strategic relevance

- low fluff

- low theatricality

- genuine but restrained warmth

The outreach should therefore:

- sound human but not chatty

- sound serious but not stiff

- sound interested but not overeager

- feel easy to send and easy to receive

**13. Outreach generation rule**

The AI should generate the full message draft, not just talking points.

The message should be:

- concise

- tailored to the contact type

- relevant to the role

- in Luis's voice

- low-friction to send

**14. Optional personalization rule**

When there is a strong, specific reason to personalize the message, the
AI may do so briefly.

Acceptable examples:

- recruiter clearly tied to the role or function

- leader clearly overseeing relevant work

- peer clearly in the same function

- real warm or semi-warm overlap

The personalization should be:

- brief

- factual

- relevant

It should not become:

- flattery

- biography commentary

- fake familiarity

**15. Core principle**

A strong outreach message should feel like:\
**a concise, intelligent note with a real reason for existing.**

Not a performance.\
Not a networking script.\
Not a plea.
