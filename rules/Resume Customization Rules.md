**Resume Customization Rules**

**Purpose**

This document defines how the AI should customize a resume for a
specific job opportunity using the approved project artifacts.

It is the single source of truth for:

- resume content sourcing

- allowed and prohibited changes

- truth constraints

- tailoring hierarchy

- structure preservation

- block-level quality control

The goal is to produce a tailored resume that is:

- strongly aligned to the target role

- grounded in the candidate evidence pack

- optimized for relevance and clarity

- truthful, disciplined, and non-fabricated

The tailored resume should represent the strongest truthful case for the
candidate in relation to the specific job description.

**1. Starting point**

The AI should always begin with the best base resume for the role.

Priority order:

1.  use the Resume Routing Guide

2.  confirm the choice against the actual JD

3.  select the strongest relevant resume variant

4.  only if none of the variants fit well should it start from the
    Master Resume

The AI should not generate a resume from scratch unless explicitly
asked.

**2. Approved source materials**

**Primary evidence sources**

- selected Resume Variant

- Master Resume

- Accomplishments Inventory

- Strengths and Capabilities Profile

- Differentiator Summary

**Secondary support source**

- Positioning and Bullet Bank

**Tertiary support source**

- LinkedIn About / Public Narrative

The tailored resume must remain grounded in these materials.

All changes should be anchored in the approved documents to prevent
expansion drift.

**3. Resume content sourcing hierarchy**

When generating tailored resume content, follow this hierarchy:

**Level 1 --- Direct reuse**

Reuse an existing bullet or positioning line exactly if it already fits
the target role well.

**Level 2 --- Adaptation**

Adapt or paraphrase an existing bullet when a shift in emphasis,
clarity, role-language alignment, compression, or sharper wording will
improve relevance.

**Level 3 --- New bullet creation**

Create new wording only when necessary and only when it is clearly
supported by the approved evidence sources.

**Core principle**

Reuse first. Adapt second. Create new only when necessary and clearly
evidence-supported.

**4. Block-level quality control**

Whenever editing a role block in the resume, evaluate the block as a
whole.

This means:

- bullets within the same role block must be distinct from one another

- bullets should not repeat the same underlying idea in slightly
  different wording

- additions, deletions, insertions, and rewrites should improve the
  strength of the block as a whole

- the final block should feel sharp, grounded, non-redundant, and
  well-composed

Optimize not just individual bullets, but the coherence and
distinctiveness of the full role block.

**5. Optimization goals**

The tailored resume should optimize for:

- alignment with the target JD

- stronger expression of relevant value

- clearer demonstration of fit

- stronger keyword and competency alignment

- better emphasis on the most relevant experience

- reduced irrelevance and noise

Optimize for relevance and clarity, not novelty for its own sake.

**6. Allowed customization areas**

The AI may tailor:

- headline / title line

- summary / profile statement

- wording of selected bullets

- order and emphasis of bullets

- inclusion or exclusion of less relevant content

- wording of relevant capabilities / skills sections

- terminology alignment to the JD, where truthful

The AI may not alter the factual core of the candidate's history.

All edits should remain anchored in the approved documents and should
not introduce unsupported expansion.

The AI may reorder bullets within a role freely for relevance and
emphasis, and may selectively trim lower-value content across the
resume, but should not substantially re-engineer the overall structure
or chronology unless explicitly asked.

**7. Prohibited behavior**

The AI must not:

- invent accomplishments

- invent metrics

- invent direct ownership

- imply technical depth not supported by evidence

- imply domain expertise not supported by evidence

- distort title history or level of responsibility

- write as though serious fit gaps do not exist

Fit gaps may be addressed through stronger translation and evidence
selection, but not through fabrication.

**8. Handling weaknesses and gaps**

The AI should distinguish among:

- Signaling Gap

- Domain Gap

- Tool / Technical Gap

- Functional Gap

- Level Gap

Guidance:

- signaling gaps can often be improved through stronger tailoring

- domain gaps may sometimes be softened through transferability

- tool/technical, functional, and level gaps generally cannot be solved
  through wording alone

The tailored resume may strengthen the case, but must not disguise
serious mismatches as resolved.

**9. Tailoring style**

The tailoring should be moderately aggressive but disciplined.

The AI should:

- sharpen the case

- foreground the most relevant strengths

- use role-relevant language

- make transferable value legible

The AI should not:

- radically reinvent the candidate

- erase the underlying truth of the career path

- mimic a "perfect candidate" at the expense of accuracy

The final resume should feel like the strongest truthful version of the
candidate for the role.

**10. Summary / profile statement instructions**

The summary / profile statement is a high-leverage section and may be
rewritten more freely than other parts of the resume, as long as it
remains clearly grounded in the approved evidence sources.

The AI should:

- align the summary tightly to the role

- foreground the most relevant strengths and value proposition

- use language consistent with the candidate's documents

- avoid generic executive-summary language

- avoid inflated or vague claims

The summary should feel:

- sharp

- specific

- aligned to the target role

- consistent with the candidate's actual profile and voice

**11. Resume output rule**

The main resume output must be a **full updated resume draft**, not
step-by-step edit instructions.

Optional notes about strategy, key changes, or residual risks may appear
if required by the **Application Output Schema**, but this document does
not own output section order.

**12. Final rule**

The tailored resume should read like the strongest truthful version of
the same person, optimized for the target role without fabrication or
drift.
